　ミントーは又アヂソンを評して斯う云つた。

    “He is the great English example of polite ridicule……Not a single paper of
Addison's can be pointed out that does not contain some stroke of malice—‘gay
malevolence,’ perhaps, but nevertheless malevolence.……Still, in characterising his
humour, the critic must not sink the fact that it is at basis malicions—it is ‘humorous
satire.’  If we call it amiable humour, we must remember that it is a kind of humour
that may be amiable to the reader or hearer, but is far from appearing amiable
to the object.”

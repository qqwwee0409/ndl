　此家具師は多少常識を失してゐる。然しながら彼の言動は只讀者をして微笑せしむるに留まる
丈である。アヂソンは是より以上のヒユーモアを好まなかつたのである。

　アヂソンは『スペクテーター』の三十五號にヒユーモアの何物なるかを論じてゐる。

    “It is not an imagination that teems with monsters, an head that is filled with
extravagant conceptions, which is capable of furnishing the world with diversions of
this nature; and yet if we look into the productions of several writers, who set up
for men of humour, what wild irregular fancies, what unnatural distortions of thought,
do we meet with? If they speak nonsense, they believe they are talking humour
and when they have drawn together a scheme of absurd, inconsistent ideas, they are
not able to read it over to themselves without laughing.”
